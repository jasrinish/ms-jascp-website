About Ms. Jaspreet

Passionate educator, programmer, and mentor helping students unlock their computer science capabilities.

My Professional Story

I hold a Master's degree in Computer Applications (MCA) and have spent over seven years teaching core programming concepts, relational database modeling, web technology structures, and data networks to students at the higher secondary and undergraduate levels.

My entry into academic pedagogy was fueled by a strong interest in demystifying syntax. Computer science is often seen as a collection of strict rules and dry equations, but it is actually a highly creative, logic-driven sandbox for making ideas a reality. My mission is to guide students from writing their first `print("Hello World")` statement to constructing scalable systems and database models.

When I'm not in front of a white board or checking student repositories, I explore recent developments in Machine Learning APIs, experiment with frontend design tools, and consult on local web dev architectures.

My Approach

Teaching Philosophy

A structured pedagogical model that moves beyond rote textbook learning to produce actual engineering problem-solvers.

Active Coding Practice

Coding cannot be memorized. My courses prioritize hands-on laboratory sessions where students compile, run, debug, and optimize real code daily.

Logical Deductions First

Instead of just demonstrating working scripts, I emphasize reading outputs, tracking variable states manually, and dry-running sorting structures.

Real-World Context

Every loop, database constraint, or networks protocol is contextualized. We explore how SQL triggers back up e-commerce portals, or how lists hold API request logs.
